Introducing Setup

As a Salesforce admin or developer, you spend a lot of time using the Setup area in Salesforce. It’s where you customize and configure your org, support users, build functionality, and more.

As you’re getting familiar with the Setup area, you’ll see that there are many ways to get to the same place in Setup. It’s not about memorizing click paths. It’s about understanding what you’re looking for to get to your destination.

Note

In this module, we’re using the original user interface, or Salesforce Classic and the mobile app SalesforceA. To learn about our new interface known as Lightning Experience, check out the Lightning Experience Features module in our Migrate to Lightning Experience trail.

And now for an important terminology note: We usually refer to the Setup area as just “Setup.” Have no fear that you might’ve missed something; it’s all the same thing.

Finding Setup

When you first log in to your org, you see your Salesforce Home page. To get to Setup, click Setup in the header, next to your name.

Meet Setup

Similar to the other parts of the application, Setup consists of a tab bar, a navigational sidebar, and a main window.

  • The tab bar [1] is made up of the same tabs that appear in the regular application. Click any tab to exit Setup and go to that tab in the main application.
  • The Setup menu [2] is comprised of expandable and collapsible lists of all the available Setup pages. These pages let you set up, maintain, and customize your org, as well as build, extend, and manage apps.
  • The main window [3] is where the navigational links or the selected setup tool are displayed.

So let’s pause here and talk about the Setup menu. You can browse for what you’re looking for on that menu, or you can use Setup Search. If you haven’t yet used Setup Search, prepare to have your mind blown.

Setup Search

Setup Search is at the top-left corner of Setup, above the left navigation menu. Setup Search uses type-ahead functionality to help you jump quickly to the item you want.

For example, if you’re looking for user profiles, you can either navigate to Administer | Manage Users | Profiles, or start typing profiles in the Quick Find box, and Profiles appears. Voila!

But let’s not stop there. Enable Advanced Setup Search (Beta) to take it to the next level. With Advanced Setup Search, you can search across multiple areas, using the same search box. Here’s how.

Let’s say you want to find a specific user. Enter part or all of the user’s name, and press Enter.

Now you know how to browse and search Setup, and as you see, you can do a lot. So what happens if you need to perform some basic setup duties when you're away from your desktop?

Take Setup on the Go

Access to Setup isn’t limited to Salesforce Classic or Lightning Experience on the desktop. If you’re not at your desk and you need to get your admin duties on, you can take Setup on the go with the SalesforceA mobile app.

You can perform essential admin tasks like resetting passwords, freezing users, and viewing current system status from your mobile device.

You can get SalesforceA for iOS and Android mobile devices. Download SalesforceA from the App Store™ or Google Play™.

Logging In to SalesforceA

Logging in to SalesforceA is as easy as pie. You need just a couple of key ingredients to log in to SalesforceA successfully.
  • Username
  • Super secret password
  • Correct instance

The last item is important. The instance can vary from production, sandbox, or a custom domain. By default, SalesforceA connects to the production environment. You can connect to a sandbox environment for testing purposes, or a custom domain if configured for your org. When you have all the ingredients ready, you’re ready to log in.

  1. Open the SalesforceA mobile app.
  2. Tap Sign In.
    • To log in to sandbox or a custom domain, iOS users tap to select the correct environment. Android users tap to select the correct environment.
  3. Enter the username and password.
  4. Tap Log In.

Monitor the Trust Status

After you log in to SalesforceA, you see the Overview page which shows the recent system status and recently viewed users.

The Recent System Status section shows information from trust.salesforce.com about your instance's system performance over the last 24 hours. Trust.salesforce.com is Salesforce’s website to provide transparency around service availability, performance, security, privacy, and compliance. Trust status gives you a quick and easy way to see if your org performance has been affected so you can let your users know of any changes.

Freeze a User from Anywhere

Pop quiz: You’re away from your desk grabbing coffee and you get an email saying a user’s Salesforce account may have been compromised. What do you do?

Well, because you have SalesforceA installed, you can calmly sip your latte and take care of business from your mobile device.

Go to the compromised user’s profile page, then tap and select Freeze.

Now the user’s compromised account is frozen to prevent any trouble, and you can get back to enjoying your well-deserved coffee break.

You’ve seen what you can do by taking Setup on the go with SalesforceA. You can monitor the trust status of your org or freeze a user whose account has been compromised. When you get back to your desk from your latte break, we’ll dive deeper into what Setup on the desktop can do for you.

Customize, Create, and Develop

You’ll spend a lot of time in the Build section [1] of Setup. Depending on your edition, you can add custom objects, automate processes, and write code for custom pages, triggers, and more.

When you drill into the Customize menu, you find a list of your standard objects, with options for customizing each one. You also see options for customizing your Home page, tabs, and more. Create and Develop both allow you to create additional customizations, using declarative and programmatic development.

We won’t dive into the details here. Check out our Trailhead modules with detailed instructions for how to use specific features in Customize, Create, and Develop.

In addition, you can manage permissions and users and monitor your overall org in several places in Setup. Next, we’ll cover some of the top pages in Setup that you’ll want to check out as you’re getting started.

Top 10 “You Can’t Miss” Pages in Setup

As you’re getting started in Setup, these are pages you don’t want to miss.

# Item What it is and why you shouldn’t miss it
1 Company Information
  • At-a-glance view of your org overall
  • Find your org ID
  • See your licenses in use and how many you have available
  • Monitor important limits, including API requests, data usage, and file usage
2 Users
  • Reset passwords
  • Manage user licenses, profiles, and permission sets
  • Create new users and deactivate or freeze existing users
3 Profiles, Permission Sets
  • Assign permissions for your overall org
  • View standard profiles and permission sets, and create your own custom ones as needed
4 View Setup Audit Trail
  • 6 months of history on changes made to your org
  • Date, time, and user stamped on who made the change
  • Although not all changes are tracked, this is an essential tool for troubleshooting issues and finding root cause
5 Login History
  • 6 months of history on who logged into your org
  • View date, time, user, IP address, browser, application, and other important data
  • Important for both security tracking purposes and adoption
6 Sandboxes
  • At-a-glance view of sandbox types, including how many are available and in use
  • List of sandboxes, their last copy (“refresh”) date, and links to log in, delete, edit, and if available, refresh
  • Sandboxes are a vital tool for administrators and developers and a worthwhile investment; if budgets absolutely do not permit, consider development in a free Developer Edition org
7 Scheduled Jobs
  • View all future jobs, including Apex jobs, dashboard refreshes, and reporting snapshots
  • Monitor a job in progress
  • Delete future jobs
8 Apex Jobs
  • Monitor the status of all Apex jobs
  • Abort a job in progress
  • Create a custom list view to see only future methods or Apex batch jobs
9 Schema Builder
  • Visual, interactive display of your standard and custom objects
  • Create objects and fields on the fly
  • Create and modify relationships between objects, all from one screen
10 Storage Usage
  • Monitor your overall file and data usage
  • View a granular list of storage by object, and top users in your org who are consuming storage limits

Getting comfortable in Setup is key to your success as a Salesforce admin or developer. Get started with Setup today!
